# Outreach — finding the economics reviewers

Copy-paste these. They're intentionally small and boring — the people who reply to boring posts are the ones who have time and prefer real work over drama.

## 1. Warpcast / X — short call for reviewers

```
Paying $300 for a 45-min review of an L1 miner-economics model.

Need someone who's designed or operated:
  • An L1 emission schedule (Cosmos / Near / Avalanche)
  • A GPU-compute marketplace (Akash / Render / Gensyn / io.net)
  • A token-emission redesign (any chain that changed after launch)

3 yes/no questions. Simple Python model. Honest writeup.
Not a pitch, not a pre-seed conversation.

Reply or DM.
```

Post on Warpcast in `/crypto-infra`, `/economics`, or `/founders`. On X, tag nobody; replies to untagged posts are higher-signal.

## 2. Cosmos Hub validator Discord — slightly longer

Drop into #general or the validator operator channel:

```
Hey — I'm running a small L1 (AI-inference chain, testnet only) and I'd
like to pay someone for 45 minutes of review on the miner-economics model.

The ask: you read a 1-page writeup + 150-line Python model, then we do a
Zoom, and you give me a "this path closes" / "this path doesn't close"
answer plus a sketch of what you'd change.

$300 flat for your time. Valid whether the answer is go or no-go.

If you've operated a Cosmos validator through an inflation-schedule change
and have opinions, I want to hear them. Reply or DM.
```

The validator channels are the most concentrated population of people who've lived through emission changes. Don't target founders; target operators.

## 3. Akash / Render / io.net Discord — mention the compute angle

```
Paying $300 for 45 min on a miner-economics review. I'm running a small

my emission schedule transitions from subsidy to fees on a reasonable
horizon, or if I should cut miner rewards now.

You've been in the GPU compute marketplace — your instinct is what I
need. Reply or DM.
```

## What to do after getting replies

1. **Ask for a 15-minute screening call** before committing the $300. You want to confirm they've actually shipped what they claim, and they want to confirm you're not wasting their time. Half of cold-outreach candidates will bail after this call — that's fine and expected.
2. **Pay upfront via crypto or Stripe** once the call is scheduled. Paying after the fact kills trust with strangers.
3. **Send [MINER-ECONOMICS.md](./MINER-ECONOMICS.md) 24 hours before the call.** Give them time to actually read. If they show up without having read it, the call is worth half as much.
4. **Record the Zoom with consent.** You'll want to re-listen when drafting the go/no-go decision.
5. **Publish their summary** (anonymized or attributed per their preference) in this directory after the review. This is the artifact that triggers the T+4 week Gap C milestone.

## Realistic reply rates

- Warpcast: 2–4 replies per 100 views, ~20% of those will follow through
- X: 0–2 replies per post, but quality is higher
- Discord: 1-2 solid replies per channel if the post is in the right channel at a non-US-sleep hour
- LinkedIn: 1 reply per 5–10 cold DMs; bail rate high on the intro call

Send all channels in parallel. Expect 2-3 weeks to convert to an actual review, not a few days. Budget accordingly against the T+4 week milestone.
